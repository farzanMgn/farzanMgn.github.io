Zerynth – Optimizing IoT Kit Efficiency

Making Everything Smarter Should Make Life Easier!

At Zerynth, an IoT company empowering factories to connect their machines, I worked on a challenge that many customers faced: the limited battery life of our IoT kits. These kits, once installed on industrial machines, continuously sent data packets every minute — even when the machine was idle, warming up, or just producing noise. This frequent transmission drained the battery, making maintenance and replacement difficult and costly for clients.

The Challenge

How can we reduce unnecessary data transmissions without losing valuable insights?

My Approach

I analyzed the power consumption patterns of 19 machines over long periods (130–364 days). By clustering consumption data into two states — Active (1) and Idle/Noise (0) — I identified when machines were truly producing useful data. During idle or noisy phases, transmissions could be safely skipped, significantly reducing unnecessary battery drain.

The Impact

  • Reduced data transmission rates by up to 35% weekly and as high as 40% monthly for older machines.
  • Extended IoT kit battery life by approximately +333 days (≈66.7% longer lifetime).
  • Lowered maintenance costs and improved customer experience for industrial clients.

This project demonstrated the power of combining data-driven insights with customer-centric thinking to solve real product challenges and deliver measurable impact.

ORC - Android Application for Low-Cost CPM Robot

Keep It Low-Cost and Scalable!

At the Orthopedics Research Center, I collaborated in a project named Orthopedic Biometrics to develop an Android application for a cost-effective Continuous Passive Motion (CPM) robot for elbow and wrist rehabilitation. At the time, only one French-made CPM rehabilitation robot (Kinetec 6080™) was available in the city at a price of $8,000, limiting accessibility for patients. This engineering team successfully designed a similar robotic device for only $600. However, the initial Android application used to interface between medical input data and the robot’s microcontroller was extremely basic, lacking usability, scalability and critical features for medical professionals.

The Challenge

The prototype robot needed a usable app for data entry, patient management, and communication with the microcontroller. The initial app was a raw data transfer tool → no clinical workflow, no patient profiles, no usability.

Research & Discovery

Users Identified:

  • Clinics, hospitals, physiotherapy centers (B2B).
  • Patients for personal use at home (B2C).

Pain Points:

  • Doctors → needed multi-patient profiles, history tracking, and structured workflows.
  • Patients → needed simple setup, personal progress tracking, and reliable control.

My Approach

Defining the Product (MVP):

I defined the core product features to validate the robot and meet user needs:

  • Dual sign-up system: Clinic/Organization( manage multiple patients with file numbers )and Individual( single patient use).
  • Profile creation: patient name, ID, DOB, medical history, visit logs, and exercise records.
  • Secure login & data protection
  • Robot data integration: rehabilitation parameters sent directly to the microcontroller.

  • Doctors → needed multi-patient profiles, history tracking, and structured workflows.
